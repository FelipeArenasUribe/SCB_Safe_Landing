Safe Position and Attitude Control for Landing on Small Celestial Bodies with Gravitational Uncertainty

Felipe Arenas-Uribe, T. Michael Seigler, Jesse B. Hoagg
Department of Mechanical and Aerospace Engineering, University of Kentucky
PREPRINT - 2026
arXiv Code BibTeX

Safe soft-landing maneuver of over-actuated spacecraft on irregularly-shaped small celestial bodies.

Abstract

Soft landing on small celestial bodies (SCBs) presents control challenges due to highly irregular gravitational fields that are poorly captured by models. For over-actuated spacecraft, these challenges are compounded by the need to manage coupled 6-DOF dynamics while respecting individual actuator limits. This paper proposes an optimal control strategy that combines disturbance estimation, trajectory tracking, and safety enforcement for fully-actuated and over-actuated spacecraft. The approach begins by mapping the high-dimensional input constraints of the over-actuated system onto the lower-dimensional space of forces and moments using a polytope projection algorithm. This allows for a unified representation of admissible control efforts. An extended high-gain observer provides real-time estimation of gravitational disturbances, while a nominal control is designed using a feedback-linearizing controller to track position and attitude reference trajectories. To guarantee safety, a minimum-intervention quadratic program utilizing Control Barrier Functions enforces state and input constraints. Finally, an optimal control allocation layer distributes the safe forces and moments back to the individual actuators. Numerical simulations on irregularly shaped SCBs demonstrate that the proposed controller achieves safe landings across various actuator configurations, offering a robust solution for autonomous space missions.
